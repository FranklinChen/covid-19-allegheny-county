  • Announcements in effect

    On Sunday, June 28, 2020, Health Department Director Dr. Debra Bogen announced that bars and restaurants are being ordered to cease on-site consumption of alcohol to help prevent the spread of COVID-19. The order is effective immediately although it will not be enforced until after 5 PM on Tuesday, June 30, 2020. Read the full order here.

    Complaints regarding establishments which are violating the Health Department order may be sent to the department after 5 PM on July 30 through

    • a webform,
    • email
    • or by calling the Information Center at 412-350-INFO (412-350-4636).

    There is also a form to report COVID-19 complaints about a business/facility to the state of PA.

    You may wish to sign up for daily updates from Allegheny Alerts for more details.

  • What does "Green Phase" really mean?

    "Green" does not mean "Go"!

    The following description of "Green" is from the official description of phases:

    Work & Congregate Setting Restrictions

    • Continued Telework Strongly Encouraged
    • Businesses with In-Person Operations Must Follow Updated Business and Building Safety Requirements
    • All Businesses Operating at 50% Occupancy in the Yellow Phase May Increase to 75% Occupancy
    • Child Care May Open Complying with Guidance
    • Congregate Care Restrictions in Place
    • Prison and Hospital Restrictions Determined by Individual Facilities
    • Schools Subject to CDC and Commonwealth Guidance

    Social Restrictions

    • Large Gatherings of More Than 250 Prohibited
    • Masks Are Required in Businesses
    • Restaurants and Bars Open at 50% Occupancy
    • Personal Care Services (including hair salons and barbershops) Open at 50% Occupancy and by Appointment Only
    • Indoor Recreation, Health and Wellness Facilities, and Personal Care Services (such as gyms and spas) Open at 50% Occupancy with Appointments Strongly Encouraged
    • All Entertainment (such as casinos, theaters, and shopping malls) Open at 50% Occupancy
    • Construction Activity May Return to Full Capacity with Continued Implementation of Protocols
    All businesses must follow CDC and DOH guidance for social distancing and cleaning Monitor public health indicators, adjust orders and restrictions as necessary
